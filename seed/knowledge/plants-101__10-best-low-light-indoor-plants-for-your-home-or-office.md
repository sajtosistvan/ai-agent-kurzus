# 10 Best Low Light Indoor Plants for Your Home or Office

Plants 101



Looking to green up your space but don’t have tons of natural sunlight? No problem. Here are our top 10 low light indoor plants that will brighten your home or workspace—without needing a sunny windowsill!

#### In This Guide, You’ll Learn:

* The 10 best low-light plants: Snake Plant, Pothos, ZZ Plant, Parlor Palm, Philodendron, Aglaonema, Dracaena, Prayer Plants, Anthurium, and Peace Lily
* Which plants are perfect for apartments, offices, or low-light corners
* Easy care tips to help your low-light plants grow and thrive

Not every room is flooded with sunlight—but that doesn’t mean it can’t be filled with plants! Low light tolerant plants love bright, indirect light just like the rest—but their superpower? They don’t need it. These adaptable green gems can handle the shade without throwing a fit, making them perfect for dim corners or spaces with minimal natural sunlight.

#### 1. Snake Plant

The Snake Plant is a true low-maintenance hero—thriving in bright light but is famously tolerant of low light and dim corners. Water it deeply every 1–2 weeks, letting the soil dry out completely in between—every 3 weeks is fine if it’s living in low light. Nearly impossible to kill, this upright beauty adds a clean, modern edge to any space. Bonus: it’s been recognized by NASA for its air-purifying powers!

#### 2. Pothos

Pothos is the ultimate easy-care trailing plant, perfect for shelves, mantels, or hanging planters. It thrives in bright, indirect light but can handle low light like a champ. Water once the soil has dried out, usually every 1–2 weeks, and watch it grow fast with minimal effort! From the creamy variegation of Marble Queen to the silvery shimmer of the Satin Pothos and the compact charm of Pothos N'Joy, there’s a variety for every plant parent.

#### 3. ZZ Plant

Looking for a plant that practically takes care of itself? The ZZ Plant is a great low light plant that only needs water every 2–3 weeks, once the soil is completely dry. Choose from the classic bright green variety or go bold with the sleek, moody Black ZZ. Its slow growth and drought tolerance make it perfect for forgetful plant parents or low-maintenance spaces.

#### 4. Parlor Palm

If you’re looking for a low-light and pet-friendly plant, the Parlor Palm is the perfect pick! This slow grower stays compact in lower light, making it ideal for small spaces and corners, but if you want to give it brighter light you’ll see it pick up the pace. Water when the top half of soil feels dry, and while it tolerates average room humidity just fine, a bit more moisture in the air will make it feel cozier. With its soft, feathery fronds and easy care, it brings a breezy, lush vibe to any room, no fuss required.

#### 5. Philodendron

Philodendrons are adaptable, forgiving plants that fit effortlessly into any space. They tolerate a wide range of lighting conditions, from bright to low light, making them perfect for any home or office space. Go classic with the trailing Heartleaf, add a pop of color with the variegated Brasil, or choose the upright Imperial Green for bold, oversized leaves.

#### 6. Aglaonema

Aglaonema's are colorful, low-maintenance plants that brighten up any room with minimal effort. Despite their vibrant foliage—like the pink-toned Wishes or the striking red Siam—they do surprisingly well in low light without losing their color. They tolerate a wide range of indoor conditions and only need water when the soil feels dry. Perfect for adding a pop of color to shelves, desks, or low-light corners.

#### 7. Dracaena

Dracaenas bring all the drama of a tree—minus the fussy care routine! Varieties like Warneckii, Lemon Lime, and Compacta Cane add bold texture and height, while still handling lower light conditions with ease. Fairly drought-tolerant, they’re happy with a drink only once the soil feels dry. Perfect for corners that need a little flair without the constant attention.

#### 8. Prayer Plants

Prayer Plants bring bold patterns and movement to your space—plus, they’re safe for your furry friends! Varieties like the upright, bushy Rattlesnake and Pinstripe add texture, while the Lemon Lime and Red Prayer Plant shine as trailing vines perfect for hanging baskets or shelves. They thrive in humid rooms, only need watering when the top half of soil is dry, and are as easygoing as they are eye-catching!

#### 9. Anthurium

If you love flowers and foliage, the Anthurium is the perfect plant to keep your space blooming for weeks on end. They’re surprisingly easygoing, thriving even in minimal light and only needing water when the soil feels dry. Their glossy leaves and vibrant blooms, ranging from red and pink to crisp white, make any room pop.

#### 10. Peace Lily

Peace Lilies bring classic elegance with their long-lasting white blooms—even in low light. They’re low-maintenance, needing water only when the soil feels dry, making them perfect for busy plant lovers. Their glossy green leaves add a fresh, calming vibe to any room.

#### Conclusion

Low-light spaces don’t have to mean dull, lifeless corners. These 10 best low-light indoor plants prove that thriving greenery can brighten even the darkest rooms. Whether you’re after bold foliage, trailing vines, or easy-care classics, there’s a perfect plant to suit your style and schedule. Still worried about not having enough light? Try out faux plants that look just like the real thing—no sunlight (or watering) required!
